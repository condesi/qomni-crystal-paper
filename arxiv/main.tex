\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{Qomni Crystal}, a full pipeline for generating domain-specific
compressed neural networks using \emph{physics as oracle} --- replacing slow language model
teachers with deterministic physical equations --- and packing the resulting weights
into a 2-bit ternary binary format (\texttt{.crystal}).
Applied to the hydraulic engineering domain (Peruvian IS.010/IS.020 standards),
we achieve \textbf{11$\times$ compression} (942\,MB BF16 $\rightarrow$ 85.3\,MB),
generate \textbf{2,000 verified training pairs in 8 seconds} at zero cost,
and deploy on-device inference with \textbf{mmap lazy loading at 0\,ms hot-swap latency}.
Our AVX2 ternary kernel with adaptive tile sizing achieves \textbf{2.2$\times$ speedup}
over float32 for large FFN layers.
The complete pipeline --- from physics equations to edge-ready binary --- runs
end-to-end in under 45 minutes on a single T4 GPU (free tier).
\end{abstract}

\textbf{Keywords:} ternary quantization, BitNet, domain distillation, physics-as-oracle,
edge inference, hydraulic engineering, Peruvian standards

\section{Introduction}

Large language models (LLMs) require substantial compute for inference, making
domain-specific deployment prohibitive in resource-constrained environments.
While quantization techniques such as BitNet b1.58~\cite{ma2024bitnet} demonstrate
that ternary weights $\{-1, 0, +1\}$ can match full-precision performance on many tasks,
the \emph{distillation} pipeline --- generating high-quality training data ---
remains the bottleneck.

Standard knowledge distillation relies on a teacher LLM.
In practice, this creates three problems:
(1)~\textbf{Speed}: Teacher models generate 1--2 tokens/second under shared load,
    making 2,000 pairs take hours;
(2)~\textbf{Cost}: API calls accumulate rapidly;
(3)~\textbf{Correctness}: LLM teachers hallucinate domain-specific numerical answers.

We propose \textbf{Physics-as-Oracle (PaO)}: replace the LLM teacher entirely with
deterministic physical equations. For engineering domains, exact governing equations
produce verified, reproducible answers at effectively zero cost and compute.

\section{The Physics-as-Oracle Framework}

\subsection{Motivation}

For domains governed by physical law, the oracle \emph{is} the equation.
Manning's equation for open-channel flow:
\begin{equation}
    Q = \frac{1}{n} A R^{2/3} S^{1/2}
\end{equation}
produces an exact answer in microseconds. No LLM required.

\section{Crystal Format Specification}

\subsection{Format Layout}

The \texttt{.crystal} binary format stores ternary-quantized weights with a compact
64-byte header enabling fast \texttt{mmap} loading:

\begin{lstlisting}
HEADER   (64B): Magic "CRYS" | Version u16 | NLayers u16 |
                ArchName [u8;32] | dims | reserved
LAYER INDEX (32B x N): layer_id | offset | n_weights | rows | cols
PAYLOAD: 2 bits/weight, MSB-first, 4 weights/byte
         00=+1  01=0  10=-1
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{AbsMean Quantization}

Following BitNet b1.58~\cite{ma2024bitnet}, we apply per-tensor absmean quantization:
\begin{equation}
    \alpha = \frac{1}{mn}\sum_{ij}|W_{ij}|, \qquad
    W_q = \text{clamp}\!\left(\text{round}\!\left(\frac{W}{\alpha}\right), -1, 1\right)
\end{equation}

\section{AVX2 Ternary Kernel with Adaptive Tiling}

\subsection{Roofline Analysis}

For a ternary matrix-vector product with 2-bit packing:
\begin{align}
    \text{OI} &= 2 \text{ FLOP/byte} \quad \text{(memory-bound)} \\
    t_{\text{layer}} &= \frac{W_{\text{bytes}}}{BW} = \frac{3.44\text{ MB}}{5\text{ GB/s}} = 0.69\text{ ms}
\end{align}
The system is completely memory-bound; reducing bytes transferred is the only lever.

\subsection{Adaptive 4/2/1-Row Tiling}

Standard 4-row tiling amortizes activation loads for tall matrices but incurs overhead
for square matrices. We introduce \emph{aspect-ratio-adaptive} tile selection:

\begin{equation}
    \text{tile} = \begin{cases}
        4 & \text{if } \text{rows}/\text{cols} \geq 2.0 \quad \text{(FFN gate/up)} \\
        2 & \text{if } 0.9 \leq \text{rows}/\text{cols} < 2.0 \quad \text{(Attn, FFN down)} \\
        1 & \text{otherwise} \quad \text{(wide matrices)}
    \end{cases}
\end{equation}

\section{Quantum-Probabilistic Inference Extension}

We propose a quantum-inspired stochastic inference mode for crystal kernels,
based on Born rule amplitude sampling.

\subsection{Mathematical Foundation}

The exact ternary matrix-vector product $y_i = \sum_j w_{ij} x_j$ can be rewritten
as an expectation under the Born rule distribution:
\begin{equation}
    P(j) = \frac{|x_j|}{\|x\|_1}, \qquad
    y_i = \|x\|_1 \cdot \mathbb{E}_{j \sim P}\!\left[w_{ij} \cdot \text{sign}(x_j)\right]
\end{equation}

The Monte Carlo estimator with $k$ samples:
\begin{equation}
    \hat{y}_i = \|x\|_1 \cdot \frac{1}{k}\sum_{s=1}^k w_{i,j_s}\cdot\text{sign}(x_{j_s}),
    \qquad j_s \sim P
\end{equation}

\subsection{Theoretical Speedup}

Setting $k = O(\sqrt{n})$ (Grover-inspired) gives:
\begin{itemize}
    \item For $n=2048$: exact $= 2048$ ops/row $\rightarrow$ quantum $\approx 45$ ops/row ($\mathbf{45\times}$ speedup)
    \item Error bound: $\mathbb{E}\!\left[|\hat{y}-y|^2\right] \leq \|x\|_1^2 \cdot \text{Var}[w \cdot \text{sign}(x)] / k$
\end{itemize}

New HTTP endpoint: \texttt{POST /qomni/crystal/quantum} and
\texttt{POST /qomni/crystal/quantum/sweep} for knee-point calibration.

\section{Deployment Architecture}

\subsection{Zero-Copy mmap Loading}

Crystal files are loaded via \texttt{memmap2} demand-paging.
Hot-swap between domains costs 0\,ms (pointer swap in a \texttt{OnceLock<Arc<Mutex<Registry>>>}).

\subsection{End-to-End Pipeline}

\begin{lstlisting}
Physics Equations (Manning, Darcy, Bernoulli, Joukowsky, ...)
  -> 2,000 verified Q&A in 8s, $0
  -> Fine-tune Qwen 2.5 0.5B (~40 min, T4 free)
  -> AbsMean quant + 2-bit pack -> hidraulica.crystal (85.3 MB)
  -> scp + register + activate  -> 0ms hot-swap
  -> AVX2 ternary inference      -> 2.2x speedup (FFN)
Total pipeline: ~45 minutes
\end{lstlisting}

\section{Conclusion}

We demonstrate that \textbf{Physics-as-Oracle} eliminates the LLM teacher bottleneck
for engineering domains, producing 2,000 verified training pairs in 8 seconds at zero
cost --- 8,000$\times$ faster than equivalent LLM generation. Combined with ternary
quantization and the \texttt{.crystal} binary format, the full pipeline delivers an
11$\times$ compressed, edge-deployable model in under 45 minutes on freely available
compute (Colab T4). The system is deployed in production on Server5 serving the
hydraulic engineering domain for Peruvian IS.010/IS.020 standards.

\textbf{Future work}: quantum-probabilistic inference calibration,
adaptive tile benchmarking, and extension to six additional Peruvian
engineering domains.
